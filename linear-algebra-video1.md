## Video 1 — Vectors, What Even Are They?

### Three Perspectives on Vectors

**Physics:**
- A vector has direction and magnitude (length)
- It lives in space, you can move it around but it stays the same vector

**Computer Science:**
- A vector is a list of numbers
- Real world example: a house described by [square meters, price]
- Each number represents a feature — from Andrew Ng's ML lectures

**Mathematics:**
- A vector is anything where addition and scaling makes sense
- Addition, subtraction, scaling — all valid operations

---

### Vector Addition

- Add two vectors → place second vector at the tip of the first
- Result: new direction, new magnitude
- Direction changes

### Vector Subtraction

- Subtraction is just addition with a flipped direction
- v - w = v + (-w)
- You reverse w, then add it to v
- Result is the vector between their tips

---

**Key insight:** Subtraction is not a separate operation — it's addition in the opposite direction.
